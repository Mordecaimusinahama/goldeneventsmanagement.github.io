Deco planning 

by Mordecai musinahamadec 08, 2021

An event decorator plans the aesthetic and atmosphere of an event. In this career, you work closely with your clients to create a theme, plan the decor, and make sure that everything is in its place. You usually work on all elements of the space, including the colors, the lighting, and other things that bring the design together. You also work with the vendors, the event planners, and other people that play a role in getting a venue together for an event. It is common to work on a freelance or contract basis in this field, so you have freedom in the events you choose to decorate.

Food is an art that can be used to create a lasting impression. No, we’re not just talking about serving lip-smacking dishes, but also putting food on display to pique your attendee’s interest. When arranged innovatively, food can bring an exciting element to your event decoration. After all, as a planner, you want your attendees to remember everything at your event, from the keynote speaker to breakout sessions to event décor! Unleash your creativity with pipe and drape for a welcoming and upscale event look. Fabric can transform your event décor by adding a depth of colour. It makes the space feel more intimate and appear bigger. To create a show-stopping experience, drape multi-coloured pennant banners or streamers from the ceiling’s centre to the corners of the room. This effect works best in small spaces with high ceilings. Whether it is the napkins or the centrepiece, think of something unique to grab the attention of your attendees. If you want to incorporate technology into your event, then using an iPad as a centrepiece is also a cool way to keep your attendees engaged. Use the iPads to display event information, presentations, or images to improve visibility for guests sitting at the back.

Ever thought about using a chair as event décor? Everyone needs chairs at an event, but that doesn’t mean they have to be arranged in the usual boring way! To make your event stand out, you can start by arranging the chairs differently. Event backdrops can be used to offer visual stimulation. It can bring your stage to life and spruce it up without overshadowing what happens on stage. By using a stage or riser, you can attract your guest’s attention and make the stage stand out. You’re only restricted by your imagination when it comes to using gifts as part of the event decor. Small goodie bags wrapped in attractive packing can be used as part of the presentation.
